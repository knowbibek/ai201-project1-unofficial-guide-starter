Noxubee Refuge is criminally underused by students considering how close it is. The boardwalk trails through the cypress are gorgeous, especially early morning when the fog is still sitting on the water. Bring bug spray, I cannot stress this enough, the mosquitoes will carry you off in summer. You can actually spot gators sunning near the lake edges so do NOT let your dog run loose and keep your distance, people forget these are wild animals. Bird people love it, tons of waterfowl in the cooler months. The longer loop trails get muddy after rain so wear shoes you don't care about. Cell signal drops in spots, tell someone where you're going. It's free, which makes it the best cheap escape when you need to get off campus and breathe. Go on a weekday and you'll basically have it to yourself.
